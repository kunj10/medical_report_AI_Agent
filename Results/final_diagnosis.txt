This report synthesizes findings from cardiology, psychology, and pulmonology consultations for patient Michael Johnson (345678). The consistent reporting of chest pain, palpitations, shortness of breath, dizziness, and sweating across all reports strongly points towards a non-cardiac etiology. The negative cardiac workup directly contradicts a cardiac origin for the described symptoms. The psychological assessment provides a strong explanation for the reported symptoms,  consistent with the presentation of panic attacks and the patient's history of anxiety. While the shortness of breath could theoretically point to a respiratory issue, the unremarkable pulmonary exam effectively rules out pulmonary pathology as the primary cause.

**Final Diagnosis:**

The occasional PVCs observed on the Holter monitor are considered incidental and not clinically significant in the context of negative cardiac testing and the overall clinical picture.

1. **Comprehensive Psychiatric Evaluation:**  A thorough psychological assessment to confirm the diagnosis of panic disorder, assess the severity of symptoms, and develop a tailored treatment plan including cognitive behavioral therapy (CBT), pharmacotherapy (SSRIs or other appropriate antidepressants), and stress management techniques.

While unlikely given the comprehensive cardiac workup, consideration of a cardiac stress test could be explored if symptoms significantly worsen or change in character.  This is primarily for reassurance rather than a suspected cardiac etiology.

**Prognosis:**

With appropriate treatment, the prognosis for individuals with panic disorder is generally good.  Early intervention and adherence to the treatment plan are essential for long-term management and improved quality of life.
